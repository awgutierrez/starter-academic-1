**Lecturer:** [Kalle Kytölä](http://math.aalto.fi/~kkytola/)      
**Teaching assistant:** Armando W. Gutiérrez   

This is a 6-week course for master students with a major in mathematics, physics, and applied
mathematics. The student learns the following: (1) to compute the expected value of a random
variable as an integral with respect to a probability measure; (2) to compute probabilities related
to independent random variables by using a product measure; (3) to recognize different types of
convergence of a random sequence; (4) to analyse sum of random variables and conditions under
it can be approximated by Gaussian distribution; (5) to represent conditional probabilities with
respect to the information content of a sigma algebra. The key topics studied in the course are
random numbers, vectors, and sequences, sigma algebras, integration with respect to a probability
measure, stochastic independence and product measure, law of large numbers and the central
limit theorem.
I held two hours of tutorials every week. I gave recitations and provided guidance to solve
homework problems. I graded homework exercises and exams.
Study material:
• Jacod, J. and Protter, P. Probability essentials. Universitext Springer, 2004.
• Williams, D. Probability with martingales. Cambridge University Press, 1991.
• Kytölä, K. Lecture notes.
