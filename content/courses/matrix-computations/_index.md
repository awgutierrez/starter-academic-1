This is a six-week undergraduate-level course for students with a major in mathematics and applied mathematics. The
student learns to analyse and solve problems in linear algebra that occur often in scientific
computing, data fitting and optimization. The course focuses on solutions of linear systems, least
squares problems and eigenvalue problems. The student learns about stability of numerical
methods. The key topics studied in the course are matrix decomposition and their numerical
computation, eigenvalue iterations, sparse matrices, iterative solution of linear systems, LU
decomposition, Cholesky decomposition, perturbation analysis, QR decomposition and Givens
rotations, fixed-point iteration, line search methods, gradient descent algorithm, orthogonal
projections and Krylov subspaces.
6
I held two hours of tutorials every week. I solved the hard exercise problems in class and gave tips
to solve homework problems. I graded homework and exams.
Study material:
• Golub, G.H. and Van Loan C.F. Matrix computations (3rd edition). John Hopkins University
Press, 1996.
• Trefethen, L.N. and Bau, D. Numerical linear algebra (1st edition). SIAM, 1997.
